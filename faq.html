Eligibility

1. How do I know if I am eligible to make a submission?

We highly encourage all firms, individuals, law schools, General Counsels and in-house teams to submit their nominations, as we have numerous categories to choose from. You are eligible to apply in the criteria of ‘Below 10 years’ and ‘Above 10 years’ experience/incorporation of the firm as on 31st October, 2020.

2. Is there a fee required to make a submission ?

Yes, nomination fee payment is required for you or your firm to participate.

Nomination Process And Assessment

1. How are the finalists chosen?

The Organisers will compile all the submitted entries and the research teams will extensively review each submission to ensure the criteria are met in order to advance to the jury members for deliberation. We stress that this process is fair, impartial and not subject to external influences.

2. How are the winners chosen?

Once the shortlist of candidates is announced, an independent judging panel will review and select their top choices for the categories they are assigned. The scores for each category will be tabulated, and the individual/firm/GC/In-House Counsel/Managing Partner/Law School with the highest score will be announced in the Forbes list.

3. What are the categories that I can take part in?

Any law firms, in-house counsels or individuals may take part in multiple categories if appropriate. Further, there are 40 practice areas in the categories of Top 50 Law Firms and Top 100 Lawyers. The categories are subject to change at the discretion of Organisers. The organisers reserve the right to move entries from one category to another if they feel it appropriate. The organisers’ decisions will be final and no correspondence will be entered into.

4. What are the Above 10 years and Below 10 years criteria?

Any law firm, in-house counsel or individual may select ‘below 10 years’ or ‘above 10 years’ of practice/incorporation. The yardstick to judge those in the ‘below 10 years’ criterion will be different than that of ‘above 10 years’ experience/incorporation of firm.

5. Would you need to have the designation of General Counsel to apply in the Top 50 General Counsel category?

Yes, but if you are the Legal Head of the company without the Designation name of General Counsel, you would be eligible to apply in this category. We would need evidence in the application that in your organisation this is the top position in the Legal department.

6. Who will be on the judging panel for Legal Powerlist 2020?

There will be an independent judging panel/jury of at least 25-30 senior and expert legal industry leaders to review the submission materials. The judging panel/jury members will be selected on the basis of their expertise and knowledge, and will be from various sectors to correspond to the diverse list of categories.

7. What will my contact information/my client’s information be used for?

All contact information provided will not be disclosed to external parties, and will only be seen by the organising team and the judges’ panel. We will keep the information on record internally, in case we need to contact you or your referral regarding any questions or discrepancies in the submission form.

8. Where will my name/firm name/team name be published if we are a finalist or winner?

As a finalist and/or winner, your firm/team/individual name will be published in a marquee edition of Forbes India print magazine. For more information, please contact us at info@legalpowerlists.com

9. Is my submission confidential?

Any information you submit will only be reviewed by the Organisers and the independent judging panel, whose names will be published. The material submitted will only be disclosed to the above; if there is any information that is strictly confidential, please mark clearly on the nomination form. Though we will do our best to maintain the security of the information you provide, any information you transmit to us is done at your own risk and the Organisers are not responsible for any unauthorized transmission of information.

3. How to nominate

Please use the online Nomination form to make your nomination.

You will be asked to provide a brief bio and photo in your nomination. The photo is not an essential requirement. It will be used, if required, for marketing purposes should your nomination be shortlisted.

Other than contact details of the nominee, you will be asked to submit a nomination statement.

  • Your nomination should be no longer than 500 words.
  • It should address the criteria as set out in the description of the category.

4. What happens to nominations?

Upon the close-off date for nominations on 15 January 2021:

  • The online application form will close.
  • >Nominations will be forwarded to all jury members in each category.
  • Judges will use an online software with a standardised template to assess nominations, with each nomination receiving a score out of 100.
  • Judging scorecards will be sent to the Organisers for consolidation on a single category scorecard.
  • The top scoring nominations will be deemed “shortlisted”.
  • All Shortlisted names will be announced in the 1st week of February.
  • The jury panel will be sent the shortlisted names for final review.
  • Jury will have a final meeting to discuss the merits of each submission.
  • Each judge will submit their final score to the Organisers.
  • The final selected names on the list will be announced in the 3rd week of February at a mega virtual event.
